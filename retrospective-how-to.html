What is a Retrospective?

A ritual gathering of a community at the end of the project to review the events and learn from experience. No one knows the whole story of a project. Each person has a piece of the story. The retrospective ritual is the collective telling of the story and mining the experience for wisdom. [KERTH]

The Retrospective Prime Directive

"Regardless of what we discover, we understand and that everyone did the best job he or she could, given what was known at the time, his or her skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand."

The Agenda

  • Set the stage
  • Gather data
  • Generate insights
  • Decide what to do
  • Close the Retrospective

Set the stage

Introduce the purpose, test the comfort level of engagement, and present the Prime Directive.

Gather data

Collect the facts or key memories to generate common understanding of what happened.

Generate insights

Focus on interpreting the data gathered in the previous phase like exploring why certain events happened or the impact an event caused to the team.

Decide what to do

Gather all the possible things to try, narrow that list down, and agree as a group which actions to try.

Close the Retrospective

Reiterate the plan of action and the possible changes. It enables the team to have something to look forward to.

The Participants

  • The Facilitator
  • The Team

The Facilitator

The person who manages the flow of the retrospective and focuses on bringing out all the information so the team can focus on just recalling the events. He/She shouldn't be joining the discussion because he/she can greatly affect the flow of ideas making it biased.

The Team

The members of the project team. Their focus is to recall the details of the events as much as possible to guide them in understanding the whole picture.

Post-Retrospetive Activities

Capture the result of the Retrospective

Document the output such as whiteboard writings or paper notes and compile them as a report. Make sure to present necessary information to put context for the other readers of the report.

Make actions visible

Check action updates regularly and actively seek action owners. Make sure that all actions chosen by the team to try are followed-up. Share the result to other teams so they could also benefit.

"Out of sight, out of mind."
